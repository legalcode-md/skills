# {{Service Name}} Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** {{Effective Date}}
**Last updated:** {{Last Updated Date}}

This policy explains how {{Company Legal Name}} ("{{Company Short Name}}", "we", "us") handles personal data in connection with {{Service Name}} (the "Service"), accessible at {{Service URL}}. It is the jurisdiction-neutral version of our privacy policy. If you live somewhere with its own data-protection statute (for example the EU, the UK, the United States, or Iceland), please read the regional variant matched to your location alongside this document.

## 1. Plain-language summary

This section is a quick orientation; the detail lives in the rest of the document.

- We collect only the personal data we need to run, secure, and improve the Service.
- We tell you what we collect, why, and how long we keep it.
- We never sell your personal data.
- We share personal data only with vendors who help us run the Service, with people you have asked us to share with, or where the law requires us to.
- You can ask us for a copy of your data, fix mistakes in it, or have it deleted. The exact rights you have depend on where you live.
- We use technical and organisational safeguards to keep your data secure, and we tell you when we change anything material in this policy.

The rest of this document expands on each of those points.

## 2. Scope & definitions

This policy applies to personal data {{Company Short Name}} processes through the Service and through related communications (email, support, marketing).

It does **not** cover:

- Third-party websites, applications, or integrations that {{Service Name}} links to or interoperates with — those are governed by the third party's own privacy notice.
- Personal data we process strictly on behalf of a business customer under a separate data processing agreement. In that scenario the business customer is the controller and you should consult their privacy notice first.

### Definitions used in this policy

- **Personal data** — any information relating to an identified or identifiable individual. This includes obvious identifiers (name, email, account ID) and less obvious ones that can identify someone in combination (IP address, device fingerprint, cookie ID).
- **Processing** — anything we do with personal data: collecting, storing, using, sharing, deleting.
- **Controller** — the party that decides why and how personal data is processed.
- **Processor** — a party that processes personal data on a controller's instructions.
- **Subprocessor** — a vendor a processor engages to help carry out the controller's instructions.
- **You** — the individual whose personal data is described.

Where regional law uses different wording for these concepts (for example "business" and "service provider" in some US state laws), we treat the regional terms as equivalent for the purposes of this policy.

## 6. Purposes of processing

We process personal data only for purposes we can justify. The main purposes are:

- **Providing the Service** — creating and authenticating your account, delivering features you request, and keeping the Service operational.
- **Account administration** — billing, invoicing, subscription management, and related record-keeping. *OPTIONAL — only if you sell paid services.*
- **Support** — answering your questions, troubleshooting issues, and following up on incidents you report.
- **Security and abuse prevention** — detecting, investigating, and stopping fraud, abuse, account takeover, spam, malicious traffic, and violations of our terms.
- **Service improvement** — understanding how the Service is used so we can fix bugs, prioritise features, and improve performance and reliability.
- **Communications** — sending transactional messages (account notices, security alerts, service updates) and, where you have opted in or where the law allows, marketing or product news.
- **Legal compliance** — meeting tax, accounting, anti-money-laundering, dispute-resolution, and other obligations imposed on us.
- **Analytics and aggregation** — generating aggregated, de-identified statistics about usage. Once data is reduced to a form that no longer identifies any individual, this policy no longer constrains its use.
- **Research and development** — *OPTIONAL — only if you use de-identified or pseudonymised data for R&D.* Improving existing features and developing new ones, using de-identified or pseudonymised data wherever possible.

We do not use personal data for purposes that are incompatible with the ones listed here without first telling you and, where required, obtaining your consent.

## 7. How we explain why we process your data

> *This section becomes "Legal bases for processing" under GDPR-family laws (EU, UK, Iceland, and others modelled on the GDPR) and "Categories of disclosure" or "Notice at collection" under US state privacy laws — see the jurisdictional variant for your jurisdiction.*

Different legal regimes ask us to justify processing in different ways. Two patterns dominate worldwide:

1. **Lawful-basis regimes (GDPR-style).** We must identify a specific lawful basis for each purpose — for example performance of a contract with you, our legitimate interests in operating a secure Service, your consent, or a legal obligation we are subject to.
2. **Notice-and-choice regimes (US-style).** We must tell you which categories of personal data we collect, the purposes we use each category for, the categories of recipients we share it with, and how long we keep it; you then have rights such as access, deletion, and (where applicable) opt-out of certain disclosures.

Wherever you live, our actual processing activities are the same: what differs is the framework we use to describe them. The summary below maps our purposes to a generic framing that both regimes can be specialised from.

| Purpose (from Section 6) | Generic justification |
|---|---|
| Providing the Service, account administration | Necessary to deliver what you have asked for. |
| Support | Necessary to respond to a request from you. |
| Security and abuse prevention | Necessary to keep the Service and other users safe; required by law in some cases. |
| Service improvement, analytics | Reasonably expected use of usage data; we minimise and aggregate where we can. |
| Marketing communications | Where you have opted in, or where soft opt-in for similar products is permitted. |
| Legal compliance | Required by statute, regulation, or lawful order. |
| R&D | Reasonably expected internal use; conducted on de-identified or pseudonymised data wherever possible. |

If you want to know the precise legal grounds we rely on for a particular activity in your jurisdiction, contact us at {{Company Email Privacy}} and we will explain.

## 9. International data transfers

The Service is operated from {{Company Country}} and uses cloud infrastructure that may store and process personal data in other countries. As a result, your personal data may be transferred across borders.

When we transfer personal data out of your home jurisdiction, we rely on one or more of the standard safeguards used in international data-protection law:

- **Adequacy decisions** — where a recognised authority has determined that the destination country provides an adequate level of protection.
- **Standard contractual clauses (SCCs)** — model contracts approved by data-protection authorities that bind the importer to specific protections.
- **International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA) or equivalent** — used where SCCs are not the applicable instrument (for example, certain UK transfers).
- **Binding corporate rules** — *OPTIONAL — only if you have approved BCRs.* Internal rules approved by competent authorities for transfers within our corporate group.
- **Your explicit, informed consent** — used only where another safeguard is not available.
- **Other derogations** — narrow, statute-specific exceptions used sparingly and only where the law permits.

If you would like a copy of the safeguard relied on for a specific transfer, contact us at {{Company Email Privacy}}. The jurisdictional variant of this policy lists the specific transfer mechanism that applies to you.

## 10. Retention

We keep personal data only as long as we need it for the purpose we collected it for, then we delete or de-identify it.

The actual retention period for a given record depends on:

- The purpose it was collected for (Section 6).
- Whether we still have an active relationship with you.
- Any legal requirement that obliges us to keep the record (for example, tax records, accounting records, or records required for dispute resolution).
- Whether we need to retain the record to defend against legal claims within the applicable limitation period.

As a guide:

| Data | Indicative retention |
|---|---|
| Account record | Lifetime of the account, plus a short tail for closure and legal limitation periods. |
| Content you create or upload | Until you delete it, or until your account is closed and the closure grace period ends. |
| Billing and tax records | The period required by applicable tax and accounting law (commonly 5–10 years). |
| Server and security logs | Short retention windows (typically 30–365 days) unless an incident requires longer. |
| Marketing preferences | Until you opt out, plus a suppression record so we honour your opt-out going forward. |
| Support tickets | A few years after closure of the ticket. |

When we no longer need personal data, we delete it or convert it into a form that no longer identifies you. Backups are overwritten on rolling cycles; data we cannot immediately purge from a backup is isolated and deleted at the next cycle.

## 11. Security measures

We use a layered set of safeguards that are appropriate to the risk involved in processing your personal data. These include:

- **Encryption in transit** — TLS for all network traffic to and from the Service.
- **Encryption at rest** — for data stored on disk by our infrastructure providers.
- **Access controls** — role-based access for staff, multi-factor authentication for privileged systems, and least-privilege defaults.
- **Audit logging** — production access is logged and reviewed.
- **Vendor due diligence** — we assess subprocessors' security posture before engaging them.
- **Backups and recovery** — encrypted backups with documented restore procedures.
- **Vulnerability management** — patching, dependency scanning, and a process for receiving and acting on security reports.
- **Incident response** — a documented procedure for investigating, containing, and notifying parties in the event of a security incident.

No system is perfectly secure. If we determine that a security incident has compromised your personal data, we will notify you and the relevant authorities within the timelines required by law.

To report a vulnerability or suspected incident, write to {{Company Email Privacy}}.

## 14. Your rights & how to exercise them

Depending on where you live, you have some or all of the rights below. The exact list, the conditions on each right, and the timelines we have to respond vary by jurisdiction; the jurisdictional variant of this policy spells out the specifics.

- **Right of access** — to obtain confirmation that we process your personal data and a copy of it.
- **Right to correction** — to have inaccurate or incomplete personal data fixed.
- **Right to deletion / erasure** — to have your personal data deleted in certain circumstances.
- **Right to portability** — to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format and, where technically feasible, to have it transmitted to another controller.
- **Right to object** — to object to certain processing, including processing based on legitimate interests and direct marketing.
- **Right to restrict processing** — to ask us to limit the use of your personal data while a question about it is resolved.
- **Right to withdraw consent** — where we rely on your consent, to withdraw it at any time. Withdrawal does not affect processing that already happened on the basis of your consent.
- **Right to opt out of certain disclosures or sales** — under some US state laws, including any sharing for cross-context behavioural advertising.
- **Right not to be subject to certain automated decisions** — see Section 15.
- **Right against discrimination** — for exercising any of the above rights.

### How to exercise your rights

Send a request to {{Company Email Privacy}}, or use any in-product mechanism we make available (for example, an in-app data export or deletion tool).

We will:

1. Acknowledge your request.
2. Verify your identity using information we already hold, to make sure we do not disclose data to the wrong person.
3. Respond within the timeframe required by your jurisdiction's law.
4. Tell you, where we cannot fulfil all or part of your request, the reason and any further steps you can take.

You can ask someone to make a request on your behalf. We may ask for evidence of their authority before we act.

You will not be charged for exercising these rights, except in the narrow cases the law permits (for example, manifestly unfounded or repeated requests).

If you are not satisfied with how we have handled your request, see Section 17 for how to raise a complaint.

## 16. Changes to this policy

We update this policy when our practices change, when the law changes, or when we add or remove features that affect personal data.

If you do not agree with a change, you can stop using the Service and, where applicable, exercise your deletion rights as described in Section 14. Continued use of the Service after the effective date of a change means you are aware of the new policy.
