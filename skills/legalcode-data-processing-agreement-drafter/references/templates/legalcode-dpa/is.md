## 1. Plain-language summary

This Section 1 is a non-binding plain-language summary; the binding terms are in Sections 2 through 19 and the Annexes. The Controller decides why and how personal data are processed; the Processor processes personal data only on the Controller's documented instructions and only for the purposes set out in the Underlying Agreement and Annex 1. The Processor will keep personal data confidential, will protect personal data using the technical and organisational measures described in Annex 2, will use only the sub-processors listed in Annex 3 (or notified to the Controller in accordance with Section 9), will help the Controller respond to data subject requests and to its other obligations under Act 90/2018 and the GDPR, will notify the Controller of any personal data breach without undue delay so that the Controller can meet its 72-hour notification duty to Persónuvernd, and will return or delete personal data when the Underlying Agreement ends. International transfers out of the European Economic Area are governed by Section 14 and Annex 4. The supervisory authority for this DPA is **Persónuvernd**, Rauðarárstígur 10, 105 Reykjavík (`postur@personuvernd.is`). Either party should read the operative sections in full before relying on them; this summary is provided as an orientation and does not modify the binding text below.

## 2. Definitions

This Section 2 defines the terms used in the DPA. Capitalised terms not defined here have the meaning given to them in the Underlying Agreement, in Act 90/2018 or in the GDPR (whichever applies to the term in context). On first use, the Icelandic statutory equivalent is given in parentheses; the same term is used in English thereafter.

- **"Act 90/2018"** means Lög nr. 90/2018 um persónuvernd og vinnslu persónuupplýsinga (*Persónuverndarlög*), as amended and consolidated from time to time on althingi.is.
- **"Controller"** (*ábyrgðaraðili*) means the natural or legal person which, alone or jointly with others, determines the purposes and means of the processing of personal data, as defined in GDPR Article 4(7) and Act 90/2018.
- **"Data Subject"** (*skráður einstaklingur*) means an identified or identifiable natural person to whom personal data relate.
- **"Data Subject Request"** means a request made by, or on behalf of, a Data Subject seeking to exercise any of the rights described in Section 10.
- **"Data Protection Officer"** (*persónuverndarfulltrúi*) means a person appointed under GDPR Articles 37–39 and Act 90/2018 to advise on, and monitor, compliance with data protection law.
- **"EEA"** means the European Economic Area, comprising the Member States of the European Union together with Iceland, Norway and Liechtenstein, governed by the EEA Agreement.
- **"GDPR"** means Regulation (EU) 2016/679 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 27 April 2016, as incorporated into the EEA Agreement by Joint Committee Decision and applied directly in Iceland.
- **"Personal Data"** (*persónuupplýsingar*) means any information relating to an identified or identifiable Data Subject that is Processed under the Underlying Agreement, as further described in Annex 1.
- **"Personal Data Breach"** (*öryggisbrestur*) means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data.
- **"Persónuvernd"** means the Icelandic Data Protection Authority established under Act 90/2018, with offices at Rauðarárstígur 10, 105 Reykjavík and contact `postur@personuvernd.is`.
- **"Processing"** (*vinnsla*) means any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, whether or not by automated means, as defined in GDPR Article 4(2).
- **"Processor"** (*vinnsluaðili*) means a natural or legal person that processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.
- **"Records of Processing"** (*skrá yfir vinnsluaðgerðir*) means the records described in GDPR Article 30 and Section 13.
- **"Restricted Transfer"** means a transfer of Personal Data to a country outside the EEA that does not benefit from a European Commission adequacy decision applicable in Iceland, as described in Section 14 and Annex 4.
- **"SCCs"** means the Standard Contractual Clauses adopted by the European Commission in Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914 of 4 June 2021, as in force in Iceland through the EEA legal order.
- **"Special Category Data"** (*viðkvæmar persónuupplýsingar*) means the categories of personal data described in GDPR Article 9(1) and corresponding provisions of Act 90/2018, including data revealing racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious beliefs, trade-union membership, genetic data, biometric data uniquely identifying a person, health data and data concerning sex life or sexual orientation.
- **"Sub-processor"** (*undirvinnsluaðili*) means any third party engaged by the Processor (or by another Sub-processor) to process Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.
- **"Telecom Act 70/2022"** means Lög nr. 70/2022 um fjarskipti, the Icelandic Electronic Communications Act, which gives effect in Iceland to obligations corresponding to the EU ePrivacy Directive.
- **"TOMs"** means the technical and organisational security measures described in Section 8 and Annex 2.

The terms "consent", "international organisation", "main establishment", "pseudonymisation" and "supervisory authority" have the meanings given in GDPR Article 4 as applied in Iceland.

## 5. Roles and responsibilities

This Section 5 sets out the parties' respective roles. The Controller is the *ábyrgðaraðili* and the Processor is the *vinnsluaðili* of the Personal Data described in Annex 1. Where the Processor independently determines purposes and means of any Processing — for example, with respect to its own corporate operations, its billing records, or its security-monitoring data — the Processor acts as a separate controller for that Processing, and that Processing falls outside this DPA.
- **5.2 Authority of the Controller.** The Controller represents that it has authority to instruct the Processor on behalf of any joint controllers, affiliates or other entities whose Personal Data are submitted to the Services, and that it has provided appropriate notice to, and where required obtained appropriate consent from, Data Subjects.

## 10. Data subject rights assistance

This Section 10 implements GDPR Article 28(3)(e) and Act 90/2018, by which the Processor must assist the Controller in responding to Data Subject Requests. Iceland's statutory rights-vocabulary is given in parentheses.

- **10.1 Forwarding of requests.** Where the Processor receives a Data Subject Request relating to Personal Data Processed on behalf of the Controller, the Processor shall not respond to the Data Subject directly (other than to acknowledge receipt and direct the Data Subject to the Controller, where appropriate) and shall forward the request to the Controller without undue delay and in any event within five (5) business days of receipt.
- **10.2 Assistance.** Taking into account the nature of the Processing, the Processor shall assist the Controller, by appropriate technical and organisational measures, insofar as this is possible, to respond to the Controller's obligations regarding Data Subject Requests. The relevant rights are:
    - the right of access (*aðgangsréttur*);
    - the right to rectification (*réttur til leiðréttingar*);
    - the right to erasure or "right to be forgotten" (*eyðingaréttur* / *rétturinn til að gleymast*);
    - the right to restriction of processing (*réttur til takmörkunar á vinnslu*);
    - the right to data portability (*réttur til flutnings persónuupplýsinga*);
    - the right to object (*andmælaréttur*);
    - the right to withdraw consent (*réttur til afturköllunar á samþykki*); and
    - the right to lodge a complaint with the supervisory authority (*réttur til að leggja fram kvörtun til Persónuverndar*).
- **10.3 Self-service tooling.** The Processor shall make reasonably available, within the Services, self-service tools that the Controller can use to access, export, correct or delete Personal Data without the need for the Processor's manual intervention.
- **10.4 Out-of-scope assistance.** Where the Processor is asked to provide assistance that is materially beyond the assistance offered by the standard Services, the Processor may charge a reasonable fee on a time-and-materials basis, provided that fees shall not be charged for assistance that the Processor is required to provide for free under Act 90/2018 or the GDPR.
- **10.5 Records.** The Processor shall keep a brief record of Data Subject Requests forwarded under this Section 10 and of any assistance provided.

## 12. Personal data breach notification

This Section 12 implements GDPR Articles 33 and 34 and Act 90/2018 with respect to Personal Data Breaches affecting Personal Data Processed under this DPA. The Processor's obligation runs to the Controller; the Controller's notification obligation runs to Persónuvernd and, where applicable, to Data Subjects.

- **12.1 Processor-to-Controller notification.** The Processor shall notify the Controller of any Personal Data Breach affecting Personal Data **without undue delay** after becoming aware of it, and in any event within **{{Breach Notification Window — default 48 hours}}** after the Processor's confirmation of the Breach, by email to the Controller's incident contact identified in Annex 1.
- **12.2 Content of notification.** The Processor's notification shall, to the extent known and as updates become available, include: (a) a description of the nature of the Personal Data Breach, including, where possible, the categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and Personal Data records concerned; (b) the name and contact details of a Processor incident point of contact; (c) the likely consequences of the Personal Data Breach; and (d) the measures taken or proposed to address the Personal Data Breach and to mitigate its possible adverse effects.
- **12.3 Controller-to-Persónuvernd notification.** The Controller is responsible for notifying Persónuvernd of a Personal Data Breach within **72 hours** of the Controller becoming aware of it, where notification is required under GDPR Article 33 and Act 90/2018; and for notifying affected Data Subjects where required under GDPR Article 34. The Processor's notification under Section 12.1 is intended to enable the Controller to meet that 72-hour window.
- **12.4 Investigation and remediation.** The Processor shall investigate the Personal Data Breach, contain and remediate it, and document the events, effects and remedial actions taken. The Processor shall reasonably cooperate with the Controller's investigation, including by providing forensic information consistent with the Processor's obligations to other customers.
- **12.5 Cost allocation.** Each party bears its own costs of breach response, except that the Processor shall bear the reasonable, documented costs incurred by the Controller as a direct result of a Personal Data Breach caused by the Processor's breach of this DPA, subject to the liability limits in the Underlying Agreement and Section 17.

## 14. International data transfers

This Section 14 governs cross-border transfers of Personal Data and is structured around Iceland's EEA framework. Detailed mechanics are in **Annex 4 (Transfer Mechanism)**.

- **14.1 EEA-internal transfers.** Transfers of Personal Data within the European Economic Area — that is, between Iceland, the EU Member States, Norway and Liechtenstein — are **not** Restricted Transfers and require no separate transfer mechanism. The free flow of personal data within the EEA follows from the EEA Agreement and from the incorporation of the GDPR into the EEA legal order.
- **14.2 Adequacy decisions.** Where the European Commission has adopted an adequacy decision under GDPR Article 45 in respect of a third country, region or sector, that adequacy decision applies in Iceland through the EEA framework, and transfers to that destination, region or sector may be made on the basis of the adequacy decision (subject to the scope and conditions of the decision).
- **14.3 SCCs (Modules 1–4).** For Restricted Transfers that do not benefit from adequacy, the parties shall rely on the **EU SCCs** adopted in Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914, as in force in Iceland through the EEA legal order. The SCCs comprise four modules (Module 1: controller-to-controller; Module 2: controller-to-processor; Module 3: processor-to-processor; Module 4: processor-to-controller). The applicable Module(s), data exporter / data importer roles, optional clauses, and Annex content for the SCCs are recorded in Annex 4.
- **14.4 United Kingdom transfers.** Transfers of Personal Data to processors or sub-processors in the United Kingdom may rely on (a) the European Commission's UK adequacy decision, as applicable in Iceland through the EEA framework, for so long as that decision remains in force, or (b) where the Processor is established in the United Kingdom and the export is governed by UK data-protection law, a separate IDTA (International Data Transfer Agreement) or the UK Addendum to the EU SCCs, as documented in Annex 4 and (where applicable) in `uk.md` of this template pack.
- **14.5 Switzerland.** Transfers to Switzerland may rely on the European Commission's Swiss adequacy decision, as applicable in Iceland through the EEA framework. Where the Processor is also subject to the Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP), it shall comply with the FADP requirements applicable to its activities.
- **14.6 Binding Corporate Rules.** OPTIONAL — only if the Processor relies on BCRs: the Processor may transfer Personal Data within its corporate group on the basis of Binding Corporate Rules approved under GDPR Article 47 by the competent lead supervisory authority and recognised in Iceland, with the corresponding authorisation by Persónuvernd where required.
- **14.7 Derogations.** Article 49 derogations are narrow and shall be relied upon only on a last-resort basis, in accordance with EDPB Guidelines 2/2018 and Persónuvernd guidance.
- **14.8 Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA).** Before relying on the SCCs or BCRs, the parties shall (where required by EDPB Recommendations 01/2020 or Persónuvernd guidance) carry out a TIA assessing the law and practice of the destination country relating to government access to data, and shall implement supplementary measures where necessary to ensure essentially equivalent protection.
- **14.9 Cross-reference.** Annex 4 sets out the operative transfer mechanism for each transfer.

## 15. Audits and inspections

This Section 15 implements GDPR Article 28(3)(h) and Act 90/2018 Article 25 by giving the Controller meaningful audit rights while providing predictable mechanics for the Processor.

- **15.1 Audit information.** The Processor shall make available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with Article 28 and Act 90/2018, including the Processor's most recent **SOC 2 Type II** report, **ISO/IEC 27001** certificate (and where applicable **ISO/IEC 27701**), penetration-test summary letters, security questionnaires, and the TOMs in Annex 2. Submission of such reports satisfies the Controller's routine audit needs unless the Controller has a reasonable, specific concern.
- **15.2 On-site or remote audits.** Where, despite the information made available under Section 15.1, the Controller reasonably requires further audit, the Controller (or a suitably qualified, independent third-party auditor on its behalf, who is not a competitor of the Processor and who is bound by confidentiality) may conduct, no more than once per twelve-month period (and additionally where Persónuvernd or another competent supervisory authority requires, or following a material Personal Data Breach), an audit of the Processor's Processing relating to the Controller. The parties shall agree in advance on scope, timing, duration, methodology and confidentiality. Audits shall be conducted during normal business hours, with reasonable advance notice (not less than thirty (30) days, save for emergencies), in a manner that does not unreasonably disrupt the Processor's operations or compromise other customers' data.
- **15.3 Cost.** Each party bears its own costs of an audit, except that the Controller shall reimburse the reasonable, documented costs of the Processor where the audit identifies no material non-compliance and exceeds the assistance described in Section 15.1.
- **15.4 Persónuvernd inspection power preserved.** Nothing in this Section 15 limits Persónuvernd's investigatory and inspection powers under Act 90/2018, including its powers to enter premises, request documents and conduct on-site inspections. The Processor shall reasonably cooperate with any Persónuvernd inspection that relates to the Controller's Personal Data and shall keep the Controller informed (subject to lawful confidentiality restrictions) where the inspection concerns Processing for the Controller.

## 16. Return or deletion on termination

This Section 16 implements GDPR Article 28(3)(g) and **Act 90/2018 Article 25(3)(g)** `[VERIFY — confirm article number against current althingi.is consolidation]`, ensuring that Personal Data does not outlive the parties' relationship without a lawful basis.

- **16.1 End-of-services choice.** On expiry or termination of the Underlying Agreement (or earlier on the Controller's reasonable written request), the Processor shall, at the Controller's election, either return to the Controller, or securely delete, all Personal Data Processed on behalf of the Controller, including from active systems and from backup systems in the ordinary course of backup rotation.
- **16.2 Return mechanics.** Return shall be in a structured, commonly used and machine-readable format reasonably specified by the Controller (or, if not specified, the Processor's standard export format).
- **16.3 Window.** The Processor shall complete return or deletion within **{{Return-or-Deletion Window — default 30 days}}** after the later of: (a) expiry or termination of the Underlying Agreement; (b) the Controller's election; or (c) any post-termination service window provided in the Underlying Agreement.
- **16.4 Backup retention.** Personal Data residing in encrypted, non-targeted backups shall be deleted in accordance with the Processor's standard backup-rotation cycle and shall not be restored to active systems other than for legally permitted purposes (such as disaster recovery during the contracted service term).
- **16.5 Retention required by law.** The Processor may retain Personal Data to the extent, and for so long as, required by Icelandic, EEA or other applicable law (for example, accounting and tax records under Icelandic law). Such retained Personal Data shall continue to be protected in accordance with this DPA and shall be Processed only for the legally required purposes.
- **16.6 Confirmation.** On request, the Processor shall provide written confirmation of return or deletion.

## 17. Liability and indemnity

This Section 17 allocates liability between the parties; it does not affect Data Subjects' direct rights under GDPR Article 82 or Act 90/2018, nor does it limit Persónuvernd's enforcement powers.

- **17.1 Allocation.** Liability between the parties under or in connection with this DPA is governed by the limitations and exclusions of liability set out in the Underlying Agreement, except as expressly modified in this Section 17.
- **17.2 Statutory liability preserved.** Nothing in this DPA limits or excludes either party's liability for matters that cannot lawfully be limited or excluded under Act 90/2018, the GDPR or other applicable Icelandic or EEA law, including statutory liability to Data Subjects under GDPR Article 82.
- **17.3 Persónuvernd administrative fines.** Each party acknowledges that **Persónuvernd may impose administrative fines** under Act 90/2018, mirroring the tiered fine structure of GDPR Article 83 (up to the higher of the percentage-of-worldwide-turnover ceiling and the relevant fixed monetary ceiling, with the Icelandic fixed ceiling expressed in Icelandic króna by reference to the EUR-denominated GDPR limits as converted from time to time) `[VERIFY — confirm current króna ceilings and FX-conversion methodology under Act 90/2018 implementing provisions]`. Each party shall bear administrative fines that are imposed on it directly by Persónuvernd, except that fines imposed on one party as a direct result of the other party's breach of this DPA shall be recoverable subject to Section 17.1.
- **17.4 Data Subject claims.** Where one party pays compensation to a Data Subject under GDPR Article 82, that party may seek contribution from the other in proportion to that other party's responsibility for the damage, in accordance with GDPR Article 82(5) and Icelandic law.
- **17.5 Indemnity.** OPTIONAL — only if the parties agree on a specific indemnity allocation: each party shall indemnify the other against third-party claims (including Data Subject claims and Persónuvernd enforcement actions) to the extent caused by that party's breach of this DPA, subject to the liability limits in the Underlying Agreement and Section 17.

## Annex 2 — Technical and organisational measures (TOMs)

This Annex 2 describes the Processor's TOMs implemented under Section 8, GDPR Article 32 and Act 90/2018 Article 27. The Processor may update these measures from time to time, provided that updates do not materially decrease the level of protection.

- **A2.1 Information-security management system.** The Processor maintains an information-security management system aligned with ISO/IEC 27001, including a documented information-security policy, defined roles and responsibilities, and a recurring management review cycle.
- **A2.2 Access control.** Role-based access control with least-privilege defaults; multi-factor authentication for personnel access to systems handling Personal Data; periodic access reviews; prompt revocation of access on role change or departure.
- **A2.3 Encryption.** Encryption of Personal Data in transit using current industry-standard protocols (for example, TLS 1.2 or higher) and at rest using current industry-standard ciphers (for example, AES-256), with documented key management.
- **A2.4 Pseudonymisation.** Where appropriate to the Processing, pseudonymisation is used to reduce the linkability of Personal Data to Data Subjects.
- **A2.5 Network security.** Segregation of production from non-production environments; firewalling and network-perimeter controls; intrusion detection and prevention; DDoS protection.
- **A2.6 Application security.** Secure development life cycle; threat modelling for new features; code review; static and dynamic analysis; periodic penetration testing by qualified third parties.
- **A2.7 Logging and monitoring.** Centralised logging of security-relevant events; integrity protection on logs; monitoring with alerting; defined retention period for security logs.
- **A2.8 Backup, availability and resilience.** Regular encrypted backups; tested restore procedures; documented business-continuity and disaster-recovery plans; defined RPO and RTO for the Services.
- **A2.9 Vendor and Sub-processor risk management.** Risk assessment of Sub-processors; contractual flow-down under Section 9; periodic review.
- **A2.10 Personnel.** Background screening within the limits permitted by Icelandic and EEA law; written confidentiality undertakings under Section 7; recurring security and data-protection training.
- **A2.11 Incident response.** Documented incident-response plan covering detection, containment, eradication, recovery, notification and post-incident review; designated incident contacts; tabletop exercises.
- **A2.12 Physical security.** For Processor-controlled facilities: 24/7 access control, monitoring, environmental controls, and safe disposal of media. For cloud-hosted infrastructure: reliance on the cloud provider's certified physical-security controls.
- **A2.13 Special Category Data.** Where Special Category Data are Processed, additional controls apply: stricter access control, stronger encryption posture, additional audit logging and (where relevant) DPIA.
- **A2.14 Certifications and audits.** The Processor maintains the following certifications and audit reports, available on request: {{ISO/IEC 27001}}, {{ISO/IEC 27701}}, {{SOC 2 Type II}}, {{Other certification}}. The parties acknowledge that these certifications, recognised internationally, are widely accepted in Iceland; **Icelandic-language certification is not required**.
- **A2.15 Change management and updates.** The Processor reviews TOMs at least annually and updates them as the threat landscape and the Services evolve.

## Annex 4 — Transfer mechanism

This Annex 4 describes the transfer mechanism for cross-border movement of Personal Data, as referenced in Section 14.

- **A4.1 EEA-internal transfers.** Personal Data may move freely between Iceland and the other EEA states (the EU Member States, Norway and Liechtenstein) without a separate transfer mechanism. Iceland gives effect to the GDPR through the EEA Agreement, and the EEA Joint Committee Decision incorporating the GDPR (and the SCCs adopted under it) into the EEA legal order means that Icelandic exports to other EEA states are not Restricted Transfers. The parties shall nevertheless apply the contractual TOMs in Annex 2 to all EEA-internal Processing.
- **A4.2 EU SCCs (Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914).** Where the Personal Data is exported from the EEA to a country that does not benefit from a European Commission adequacy decision applicable in Iceland, the parties shall execute the **EU SCCs**, as in force in Iceland through the EEA legal order. The selection guide is:
    - **Module 1 (controller-to-controller)** applies where the data exporter and data importer are both controllers — for example, where the Controller and the Processor share Personal Data for distinct controller purposes.
    - **Module 2 (controller-to-processor)** applies to most Restricted Transfers under this DPA, where the Controller (data exporter) instructs the Processor (data importer) to Process Personal Data outside the EEA.
    - **Module 3 (processor-to-processor)** applies where the Processor (data exporter) onwardly transfers Personal Data to a Sub-processor (data importer) outside the EEA.
    - **Module 4 (processor-to-controller)** applies in the relatively rare case where the data exporter is a processor and the data importer is a controller (for example, return of Personal Data to a non-EEA controller).
    For each transfer, the parties shall complete Annexes I, II and III to the SCCs, select Clause 7 (docking) where the parties agree to allow third-party accession, select Option 2 (general written authorisation) under Clause 9(a) to align with Section 9 of this DPA where Module 2 or 3 applies, and select Option 1 or Option 2 under Clause 17 (governing law) in line with Section 18. Where the Controller also operates an EU establishment, cross-reference `eu.md` of this template pack for the EU-side execution.
- **A4.3 United Kingdom transfers.** Where Personal Data is transferred to or from the United Kingdom and the transfer is governed by UK data-protection law, the parties shall execute the **IDTA** or the **UK Addendum to the EU SCCs**, as documented in `uk.md` of this template pack. Where the transfer remains governed by EEA law and the United Kingdom benefits from the European Commission's UK adequacy decision, no additional mechanism is required for the duration of that decision.
- **A4.4 Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA).** Where the parties rely on the SCCs (or BCRs), they shall, where required by EDPB Recommendations 01/2020 (Recommendations on supplementary measures) or by Persónuvernd guidance, carry out a documented assessment of the law and practice of the destination country relating to government access to data, and shall implement supplementary technical, contractual and organisational measures where necessary to ensure essentially equivalent protection. The Processor shall reasonably assist the Controller's TIA on request.
- **A4.5 Binding Corporate Rules.** Where the Processor (or its corporate group) holds approved BCRs under GDPR Article 47, intra-group transfers may be carried out on the basis of those BCRs, with the corresponding **Persónuvernd authorisation** where required for Iceland-related transfers.
- **A4.6 Derogations (Article 49).** Reliance on derogations is narrow and last-resort, and shall be documented in writing in accordance with EDPB Guidelines 2/2018 and Persónuvernd guidance. In particular: derogations are not appropriate for routine, repetitive or large-volume transfers, and shall be used only where no adequacy decision and no Article 46 mechanism is available.
- **A4.7 Switzerland.** Transfers to Switzerland may rely on the European Commission's Swiss adequacy decision, as applicable in Iceland through the EEA framework. Where the Processing is also governed by the FADP, the Processor shall comply with FADP requirements applicable to its activities (including, where relevant, FADP-specific addenda to the SCCs published by the Swiss Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner).
