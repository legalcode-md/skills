## 1. Plain-language summary

*Plain-language: A short overview of how cookies work, what Icelandic law requires, and what choice you have.*

Cookies (vafrakökur) and similar technologies are small files or data slots placed on, or read from, your device. Some are required for the Service to work at all; others are optional and run only with your permission.

In Iceland, the rules come from two sources. **Lög nr. 70/2022 um fjarskipti** implements the EEA-applicable ePrivacy rule — equivalent to Article 5(3) of Directive 2002/58/EC — which requires informed consent before any non-essential information is stored on, or read from, a user's terminal equipment `[VERIFY exact section number for cookie/device-storage consent in Lög nr. 70/2022]`. **Act 90/2018 Article 9** and **GDPR Article 6(1)(a)** govern the *quality* of that consent: freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous, by a clear affirmative act.

In short: required cookies run automatically (no consent needed); optional cookies (functional, analytics, advertising, social) run only after you say yes via our consent banner; you can change your mind at any time at {{Cookie Settings URL}} or via the persistent footer link; you can complain to **Persónuvernd** (the Icelandic Data Protection Authority).

## 2. What cookies and similar technologies are

*Plain-language: "Cookies" is shorthand for several related technologies that store or read information on your device.*

Lög nr. 70/2022 and the EEA-incorporated ePrivacy Directive apply by reference to *any* storage of, or access to, information already on a user's terminal equipment — not just text files labelled "cookie". The European Data Protection Board ("EDPB") confirmed this broad reading in **Guidelines 2/2023 on the technical scope of Article 5(3)**, which apply in Iceland via the EEA Joint Committee mechanism `[VERIFY EEA-incorporation status of EDPB Guidelines 2/2023]`.

- **Cookies (vafrakökur)** — small text files placed on a device and read back on later visits. *Session* cookies disappear when the browser closes; *persistent* cookies survive until they expire or are deleted.
- **Local and session storage (geymsla á tæki)** — larger browser data slots used to keep state between page loads.
- **Web beacons and pixels (vefmælingapunktar)** — invisible images or scripts that report when a page or email is opened.
- **Device fingerprinting (auðkenningar á tæki)** — device characteristics (screen, fonts, time zone, language) that, combined, can identify a returning visitor without a stored cookie. Per EDPB Guidelines 2/2023, fingerprinting falls inside the consent rule.
- **Mobile SDKs** — libraries embedded in mobile apps that read or write similar information.

Where this policy says "set a cookie", read it as "set a cookie or use any equivalent storage or access technology listed above".

## 4. Categories of cookies we use

*Plain-language: We sort cookies into five categories. The first runs automatically; the other four run only after you opt in.*

The five categories below map to the consent regime under Lög nr. 70/2022 and EEA ePrivacy.

### 4.1 Strictly necessary (nauðsynlegar vafrakökur)

Required for the Service to function; cannot be switched off. Set in response to actions you take (logging in, filling a form, recording a consent choice). Examples: session identifiers, authentication tokens, CSRF tokens, load-balancer affinity cookies, the cookie-consent record. **Consent under Lög nr. 70/2022: not required** — these fall within the carrier exception for storage or access "strictly necessary in order to provide an information society service explicitly requested by the user" `[VERIFY exact wording and section in Lög nr. 70/2022]`, equivalent to the second exemption in ePrivacy Art. 5(3). **Legal basis:** Art. 6(1)(b) contract performance or 6(1)(f) legitimate interests for associated personal-data processing.

### 4.2 Functional / preferences (virkni- og stillingakökur)

Remember choices you make for a more personalised experience. Examples: language and locale, theme, region, accessibility settings. **Consent: required.** **Legal basis:** consent (samþykki).

### 4.3 Analytics / performance (mælingakökur)

Help us understand how visitors interact with the Service in aggregate. Examples: unique visitor counts, page-view counts, navigation paths, error reports, performance timings. **Consent: required.** The narrow EDPB "audience-measurement" carve-out (first-party, aggregate-only, no third-country transfer, no profile-building) is read strictly; in current Persónuvernd practice it is rarely available where a third-party analytics tool is used `[VERIFY current Persónuvernd cookie guidance]`. **Legal basis:** consent.

### 4.4 Advertising / marketing (auglýsinga- og markaðskökur)

Set by us or by third-party advertising partners to build a record of your interests so that advertising is more relevant. Examples: conversion pixels, retargeting tags, frequency-cap counters, click-attribution identifiers. **Consent: required**, no exemption available. {{[OPTIONAL — only if advertising cookies present; otherwise replace with: "We do not use advertising or marketing cookies."]}}

### 4.5 Social media (samfélagsmiðlakökur)

Set by social networks whose embeds, share buttons, or login widgets appear on the Service. Examples: embedded video, share-button, and social-login cookies. **Consent: required.** **Joint-controllership flag:** where a social network sets cookies through an embed for its own purposes, the operator and the network are typically **joint controllers (sameiginlegir ábyrgðaraðilar)** under GDPR Art. 26 (following *Fashion ID*). {{[OPTIONAL — only if social embeds present]}}.

## 9. Global Privacy Control (GPC) & Do Not Track (DNT)

*Plain-language: Persónuvernd has not endorsed GPC as a binding signal under Icelandic law. We treat GPC as a non-binding preference and DNT as informational only.*

**Global Privacy Control (GPC).** GPC is a signal a browser or extension sends to indicate that the user does not want their personal information sold or shared, and wants to opt out of targeted advertising. **Persónuvernd has not, as of the last-updated date of this policy, formally endorsed GPC as a binding opt-out signal under Lög nr. 70/2022 or Act 90/2018** `[VERIFY current Persónuvernd position on GPC]`. We nonetheless treat GPC as a **non-binding preference**: where a request carries a GPC header, we apply the equivalent of "reject all non-essential" on a best-effort basis. Where applicable law in another jurisdiction (e.g., certain US states) treats GPC as binding, we honour it as such.

**Do Not Track (DNT).** DNT was never adopted as a binding standard in the EEA or Iceland. Lög nr. 70/2022 does not make DNT a recognised consent signal. We treat DNT as **informational only**.

## 12. Children's data

*Plain-language: We do not use non-essential cookies to track children, and targeted advertising is not directed at children.*

Iceland sets the **age of digital consent at 13** under **Act 90/2018, Article 17**, exercising the GDPR Art. 8 national-discretion margin (the GDPR baseline is 16 unless a Member State sets a lower age between 13 and 16) `[VERIFY Act 90/2018 Art. 17 digital-consent age and current text]`.

For visitors **aged 13 or over**, the consent regime in §10 applies in the ordinary way. For visitors **under 13**, we **suppress all non-essential cookies** regardless of consent state; any GDPR Art. 8 consent must be given or authorised by the holder of parental responsibility. We do not direct **targeted advertising**, **behavioural analytics**, or **fingerprinting** at users we know or reasonably suspect to be children. Where the Service has age-gating or a user self-identifies as below 13, non-essential cookies are blocked at the consent layer.

## Annex B — Iceland Consent banner UX requirements

*Plain-language: This annex is for the team building the consent banner. It sets the floor under Lög nr. 70/2022 and Persónuvernd guidance.*

Where the jurisdiction-neutral baseline in `core.md` Annex B is stricter, the stricter rule wins.

**B.1 Bilingual labels.** All controls carry **bilingual labels of equal prominence**: "Samþykkja allt" / "Accept all"; "Hafna öllu" / "Reject all"; "Stilla valkosti" / "Manage preferences"; "Stilla vafrakökur" / "Cookie settings" (footer link). Same size, contrast, and affordance type. Where the user's language is Icelandic, the Icelandic label is primary; where English, English is primary; the other language remains visible.

**B.2 First-visit behaviour.** Banner appears on the first visit, before any non-essential cookie is set. Strictly-necessary cookies under §4.1 may be set in advance.

**B.3 Equal prominence and no dark patterns.** "Accept all" and "Reject all" share the same visual weight. **No pre-ticked boxes** for any non-essential category. No nudge colours, no buried reject control, no friction asymmetry, no auto-scroll past the choice.

**B.4 Granular per category.** Independent grant/withhold per category (§4), with one-line plain-language purpose descriptions.

**B.5 Persistent settings access.** Persistent **footer link** to the cookie settings centre on every page where a cookie may be set.

**B.6 Consent record retention.** Each consent event logged (pseudonymous identifier, UTC timestamp, banner version, language, categories accepted/rejected, mechanism). Retained **at least 12 months**, tamper-evident.

**B.7 Re-prompt cadence.** When a category materially changes, and at least every **12 months** by default. Do not re-prompt purely to wear the user down.

**B.8 Withdrawal parity.** Withdrawing consent is no harder than granting it.

**B.9 Cookie-wall justification (restrictive).** A "cookie wall" is, by default, **not permitted** under EDPB Guidelines 05/2020 and Persónuvernd guidance because consent cannot be freely given. Use one only where there is a compelling, documented justification (e.g., an editorially equivalent paid alternative) reviewed by qualified counsel.

**B.10 Accessibility.** WCAG 2.2 AA: keyboard-operable, screen-reader-labelled, sufficient contrast, no time-out on the choice. Plain-language copy at a reading level no higher than {{Reading Level Target}} (default: B1) in both Icelandic and English.

**B.11 Signal handling.** Treat GPC per §9 (non-binding preference, applied best-effort as "reject all non-essential"). Treat DNT as informational only. A returning user's prior opt-out is respected on first load.
