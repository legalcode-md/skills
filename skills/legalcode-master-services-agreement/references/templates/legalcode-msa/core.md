## 1. Plain-language summary

This Section explains, in non-binding plain English, what the rest of
the Agreement does so a business reader can orient quickly.

{{Provider Short Name}} agrees to deliver professional services to
{{Client Short Name}} under one or more Statements of Work (each, an
"SOW") or Order Forms attached to this Agreement. This document
("MSA") sets the legal and commercial baseline that governs every
SOW: how engagements get scoped, how money moves, who owns the work
product, what each Party promises, what happens when something goes
wrong, and how the relationship ends. The SOWs carry the
deal-specific particulars — what is being built, by when, for how
much, and against what acceptance criteria. If anything in an SOW
conflicts with this MSA, this MSA controls unless the SOW expressly
identifies the conflict and says it overrides.

If you are reading this clause, treat it as a roadmap, not as the
operative terms. The numbered sections that follow are what bind the
Parties.

## 2. Definitions

This Section assigns precise meanings to capitalised terms used
throughout the Agreement so the operative clauses read consistently.

For purposes of this Agreement, the following terms have the meanings
given:

**"Acceptance Criteria"** — objective standards for judging
Deliverable conformity, set in the SOW or, absent that, in Annex C.

**"Affiliate"** — an entity that, directly or indirectly, controls,
is controlled by, or is under common control with a Party (>50%
voting equity or equivalent direction power).

**"Background IP"** — IP owned by, licensed to, or independently
developed by a Party before or independently of an SOW.

**"Business Day"** — any day other than Saturday, Sunday, or public
holiday at Provider's principal place of business.

**"Change Order"** — a written SOW amendment signed by both Parties
under Section 6.

**"Confidential Information"** — as defined in Section 10.

**"Deliverable"** — any output the SOW expressly identifies as a
deliverable (software, documentation, designs, reports, data,
configurations).

**"Effective Date"** — the date stated at the top of this Agreement.

**"Fees"** — amounts payable by Client to Provider under an SOW.

**"Foreground IP"** — IP created, conceived, or first reduced to
practice by Provider (alone or with Client) in performing an SOW.

**"Intellectual Property"** or **"IP"** — all intangible rights,
registered or unregistered: patents, copyrights, design rights,
database rights, trademarks, trade secrets, know-how, moral rights,
and applications/extensions of the foregoing.

**"Key Personnel"** — individuals identified as such in an SOW or
Annex B.

**"Order Form"** — an ordering document incorporating this Agreement
and describing recurring Services pricing/terms; treated as an SOW
unless expressly distinguished.

**"Personal Data"** — information relating to an identified or
identifiable natural person.

**"Services"** — the professional, advisory, implementation,
managed, or other services in an SOW.

**"SOW"** or **"Statement of Work"** — a document executed under
Section 4 scoping a discrete engagement; Order Forms are SOWs unless
distinguished.

**"Subcontractor"** — any third party engaged by Provider to perform
Services, other than Provider's or its Affiliates' employees.

**"Third-Party Materials"** — third-party-owned materials
incorporated into or required to use a Deliverable (open-source
software, commercial libraries, fonts, stock, data feeds).

Words importing the singular include the plural and vice versa.
Headings are for convenience only and do not affect construction.
References to a statute include any subordinate legislation made
under it and any successor legislation; the Parties acknowledge
that, in this jurisdiction-neutral baseline, no specific statute is
cited and that the regional companion documents will identify the
governing instruments.

## 3. Engagement structure (MSA + SOW / Order Form framework)

This Section explains how the MSA and the SOWs fit together and which
document controls when they appear to conflict.

3.1 **Framework agreement.** This Agreement is a framework. By
itself, it imposes no obligation on Provider to perform Services and
no obligation on Client to purchase Services. Each engagement is
formed by a separately executed SOW.

3.2 **Incorporation.** Each SOW incorporates this Agreement by
reference. By signing, countersigning, or otherwise approving an SOW
through the method designated as {{SOW Approval Method}}, each Party
agrees that the SOW is governed by this Agreement.

3.3 **Order of precedence.** In the event of an inconsistency among
the contract documents, the order of precedence is:
(a) any Change Order, in respect of the SOW it amends;
(b) the body of this Agreement;
(c) the body of the SOW; and
(d) any annex, exhibit, or schedule attached to either.

The SOW may override a particular provision of this Agreement only
where the SOW expressly identifies the provision being overridden
and states that it supersedes that provision for the SOW in
question. A general "in the event of conflict, the SOW controls"
clause is not sufficient and is of no effect.

3.4 **Independent SOWs.** Each SOW is an independent contract for
performance and payment purposes; the termination, suspension, or
breach of one SOW does not, of itself, terminate, suspend, or
constitute a breach of any other SOW, except as provided in
Sections 13, 14, 16, or where a single underlying breach affects
multiple SOWs.

3.5 **No master commitment.** Unless an SOW states otherwise,
nothing in this Agreement requires Client to engage Provider on an
exclusive basis, nor does it grant Provider any minimum spend,
right of first refusal, or guaranteed volume.

3.6 **Affiliates.** A Party's Affiliate may execute an SOW under
this Agreement, in which case the Affiliate is treated as the
"Provider" or "Client" for that SOW only, and the original
signatory remains jointly liable for its Affiliate's obligations
under that SOW.

## 4. Scope of services & SOW process

This Section sets out how the Parties create, modify, and finalise
the description of work that an SOW is meant to capture.

4.1 **SOW contents.** Each SOW will, at a minimum, specify:
(a) a description of the Services and any Deliverables;
(b) the timeline, milestones, and any acceptance gates;
(c) the Fees and the basis on which they are calculated (fixed
fee, time-and-materials, milestone-based, or recurring);
(d) the Key Personnel, if any;
(e) Acceptance Criteria where Deliverables are produced;
(f) any assumptions on which Provider has based its estimates;
(g) any Client-furnished materials, data, or access on which
Provider's performance depends; and
(h) any deviations from this Agreement, identified clause by
clause as required by Section 3.3.

4.2 **Estimates.** Estimates of effort, duration, or cost given
outside an executed SOW are non-binding. Provider will, however,
exercise reasonable professional skill in producing them.

4.3 **Provider obligations.** Provider will perform the Services
with the degree of skill and care reasonably expected of a
competent professional performing services of a similar type. Where
a Deliverable is produced, Provider will use commercially reasonable
efforts to deliver it on the schedule set out in the SOW.

4.4 **Client cooperation.** Client will provide the access,
information, decisions, and approvals identified in the SOW or
reasonably necessary for Provider to perform. Provider's
performance is excused to the extent it is delayed or prevented by
Client's failure to do so, provided Provider gives prompt written
notice of the impediment.

4.5 **Reliance on Client information.** Provider may rely on the
accuracy and completeness of information furnished by Client. The
Services and any Deliverables produced are no better than the
information on which they are based, and Provider has no obligation
to independently verify Client information except where the SOW
expressly so provides.

4.6 **Reporting cadence.** Unless the SOW says otherwise, Provider
will provide Client with reasonable written or oral status reports
no less than monthly during active performance.

4.7 **Project management.** Each Party will designate a single
project manager for each SOW. A Party may change its project manager
on written notice; the new manager assumes the prior manager's
authority on the date stated in the notice.

## 5. Fees, expenses & invoicing  *(DUAL-PERSPECTIVE)*

This Section governs how Provider charges, how Client pays, and what
each Party can do when payment goes wrong. The Parties select one of
the three variants below at signing.

### 5.A Client-favorable variant

5.A.1 **Fees.** Client pays the Fees in each SOW, in {{Currency}}.
Fees are inclusive of all costs of performance (travel, lodging,
materials, overhead) except for pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses
substantiated by receipts.

5.A.2 **Invoicing.** Provider invoices monthly in arrears against
work actually completed, with reasonable detail: dates, personnel,
description, hours (where applicable), and the milestone or
Deliverable. Invoices lacking this detail are not "properly
submitted."

5.A.3 **Payment terms.** Properly submitted, undisputed invoices are
payable {{Payment Terms}} after Client's receipt.

5.A.4 **Disputed amounts.** Client may withhold any invoice item
disputed in good faith, on written notice within fifteen (15) days
of receipt identifying the basis. The undisputed portion remains
payable on time.

5.A.5 **Late fees and suspension.** Provider may not assess late
fees, interest, or suspension on disputed invoices. On undisputed
invoices unpaid more than fifteen (15) days past due, Provider may
charge interest at the lesser of {{Late Fee Rate}} or the maximum
permitted by law.

5.A.6 **MFC pricing.** For comparable scope, volume, and term,
Provider's rates to Client will not exceed those offered to any
similarly situated customer.

5.A.7 **No mid-term increases.** Rates are fixed for the SOW
duration; renewal pricing is subject to Section 16.

5.A.8 **Audit.** Once per twelve (12) months on reasonable notice,
Client (or its independent auditor under Section 10-equivalent
confidentiality) may audit Provider's books relevant to Fees.
Provider reimburses audit costs and refunds overcharges where the
audit reveals overcharges greater than three percent (3%).

5.A.9 **Taxes.** Fees are exclusive of indirect or transactional
taxes. Each Party bears taxes on its own net income.

### 5.B Provider-favorable variant

5.B.1 **Fees.** Client pays the SOW Fees. For T&M work, Provider's
then-current standard rates apply unless a fixed rate card is
incorporated. Fees are in {{Currency}}, exclusive of all taxes
(other than on Provider's net income).

5.B.2 **Expenses.** Reasonable, documented out-of-pocket expenses
are reimbursable in addition to Fees, subject to Client's published
T&E policy or, absent one, customary professional-services norms.

5.B.3 **Invoicing.** Provider invoices: (i) on SOW execution for
mobilisation/set-up fees stated as due on signing; (ii) monthly in
advance for recurring Services; (iii) monthly in arrears for T&M.
Invoices are payable {{Payment Terms}} after invoice date.

5.B.4 **Disputed amounts.** A disputing Party must notify within
ten (10) days of receipt; failing which the invoice is deemed
accepted. The undisputed balance remains payable; Section 18
governs resolution.

5.B.5 **Late fees and suspension.** Late payment bears interest at
{{Late Fee Rate}} from the due date, daily. If undisputed amounts
remain unpaid more than {{Cure Period}} days past due, Provider
may, on five (5) days' further notice, suspend Services across all
SOWs until arrears (with interest) clear. Suspension does not toll
Client deadlines.

5.B.6 **Annual rate adjustment.** On each anniversary, Provider may
increase rates by no more than the greater of (a) five percent (5%)
and (b) CPI change since the prior adjustment for Provider's
jurisdiction. [VERIFY: that the chosen CPI index publishes on the
dates required.]

5.B.7 **Collection costs.** Client reimburses Provider's reasonable
collection costs, including legal fees, on amounts ultimately
determined to be properly due.

5.B.8 **Set-off.** Client may not set off, withhold, or
counterclaim except for amounts disputed under Section 5.B.4.

### 5.C Market-standard variant *(default)*

5.C.1 **Fees.** Client pays the SOW Fees in {{Currency}}.

5.C.2 **Invoicing.** Provider invoices (i) fixed-fee work per the
SOW milestone or billing schedule; (ii) T&M work monthly in
arrears; (iii) recurring Services in advance per the SOW cadence.
Invoices include reasonable detail of work and any expenses
claimed.

5.C.3 **Expenses.** Reasonable documented out-of-pocket expenses
are reimbursable where the SOW so provides or Client has
preapproved in writing. Premium travel and entertainment require
express written approval.

5.C.4 **Payment terms.** Properly submitted undisputed invoices are
payable {{Payment Terms}} after invoice date.

5.C.5 **Disputes.** A disputing Party gives written notice within
fifteen (15) days of receipt identifying line items and basis. The
undisputed balance remains payable; Section 18 resolves the
dispute.

5.C.6 **Late fees.** Late payment of undisputed amounts bears
interest at {{Late Fee Rate}} from the due date. If undisputed
Fees remain unpaid more than {{Cure Period}} days past due,
Provider may, on prior written notice, suspend Services under the
affected SOW until arrears clear.

5.C.7 **Annual rate adjustment.** On each anniversary, Provider may
adjust standard rates upward on no less than sixty (60) days'
notice. For SOWs with a fixed fee, the adjustment applies only to
subsequent SOWs or Change Orders.

5.C.8 **Taxes.** Fees are exclusive of indirect or transactional
taxes (VAT, sales tax). Each Party bears taxes on its own net
income.

5.C.9 **Currency.** Where Fees are denominated in a currency other
than the legal tender of Client's place of payment, Client bears FX
risk and conversion charges.

## 6. Change control & change orders  *(DUAL-PERSPECTIVE)*

This Section governs how a previously agreed scope, schedule, or
price gets changed mid-engagement.

### 6.A Client-favorable variant

6.A.1 **Right to direct changes.** Client may at any time, by
written notice, direct an addition, deletion, or modification to
the scope of any SOW. Provider will, within five (5) Business Days,
return a written impact analysis describing the effect (if any) on
schedule, Fees, and Deliverables.

6.A.2 **No price gouging.** Any Fee adjustment must be based on
Provider's then-published rate card, applied consistently with the
unit of work. Provider may not condition acceptance of a change on
a renegotiation of unrelated terms.

6.A.3 **Continuation pending agreement.** Provider will continue
to perform the unaffected portions of the SOW while a change is
under negotiation. Provider may not stop work or refuse the change
direction except where the change requires capabilities Provider
does not possess.

6.A.4 **Out-of-scope work without written approval.** Provider
performs out-of-scope work at its own risk; Client is not obligated
to pay for work performed outside an executed SOW or Change Order,
even if Client benefits from the work.

6.A.5 **Disputes over change scope.** If the Parties cannot agree
that a directed item is or is not within the existing scope,
Provider will perform under protest pending escalation under
Section 18, and Client will reserve the disputed Fees pending
resolution.

### 6.B Provider-favorable variant

6.B.1 **Mutual agreement required.** No change to an SOW takes
effect except by a written Change Order signed by both Parties. A
Party requesting a change submits the request in writing; Provider
returns an impact analysis with a price and schedule effect within
ten (10) Business Days.

6.B.2 **Scope creep.** Where Client requests, directs, or accepts
work that is outside the SOW scope, that work is treated as a
verbal change request: Provider may invoice for the work performed
at its standard rates, and Client will pay the resulting invoice as
if a Change Order had been signed. Acquiescence by Client's
project manager constitutes acceptance.

6.B.3 **Assumptions.** SOW Fee estimates rest on the assumptions
stated in the SOW. If an assumption proves materially incorrect,
Provider may issue a Change Order request to true up scope and
Fees; if Client declines, Provider may suspend or terminate the
affected SOW on thirty (30) days' written notice.

6.B.4 **Right to refuse changes.** Provider may refuse a change
request that is technically infeasible, would compromise quality,
would breach a third-party licence, or for which Provider lacks
the personnel or resources at the time requested.

6.B.5 **Mobilisation costs.** A Change Order may include
mobilisation, ramp-up, or transition costs reasonably necessary
to accommodate the change.

### 6.C Market-standard variant *(default)*

6.C.1 **Process.** Either Party may request a change by written
notice. Within ten (10) Business Days, Provider will return a
written Change Order proposal stating the impact on scope,
schedule, Deliverables, and Fees.

6.C.2 **Effect.** A Change Order takes effect when signed by both
Parties' authorised representatives or approved through the method
designated as {{SOW Approval Method}}. Until then, the existing
SOW remains in effect.

6.C.3 **No verbal changes.** Oral instructions, emails between
project managers, or informal acceptance of out-of-scope work do
not change an SOW. Each Party will instruct its personnel
accordingly.

6.C.4 **Continuation pending agreement.** Provider continues to
perform the existing SOW while a Change Order is being negotiated,
unless the change is incompatible with continued performance.

6.C.5 **Pricing for changes.** Where Fees are not specified in the
Change Order, the work will be priced at the time-and-materials
rate card in the SOW, or in its absence at Provider's then-current
standard rates.

6.C.6 **Documentary record.** All executed Change Orders are
attached to, and form part of, the SOW they amend.

## 7. Personnel, key personnel & subcontracting

This Section deals with who actually performs the Services and what
restrictions apply to their replacement, removal, or substitution.

7.1 **Provider's personnel.** Provider is responsible for the acts
and omissions of its employees, contractors, and Subcontractors in
the performance of the Services. All personnel must be qualified
and adequately trained for their assigned tasks.

7.2 **Independent contractor relationship.** The Parties are
independent contractors. Nothing in this Agreement creates a
partnership, joint venture, agency, employment, fiduciary, or
exclusive relationship. Neither Party has authority to bind the
other except as expressly provided. Provider's personnel are not
employees of Client and are not entitled to Client benefits, equity,
or status.

7.3 **Worker classification.** Provider acknowledges responsibility
for proper classification, payment, withholding, and reporting in
relation to its personnel under all applicable employment, tax, and
social-insurance laws. Provider will indemnify Client for any
liability arising from Provider's misclassification of its
personnel, except to the extent caused by Client's direction.
Worker-classification specifics for the relevant jurisdiction are
addressed in the regional companion document.

7.4 **Key Personnel.** Where an SOW or Annex B identifies Key
Personnel, Provider will not remove, reassign, or materially reduce
the time commitment of those individuals during the term of the
relevant SOW without Client's prior written consent (not to be
unreasonably withheld). Replacement (if Client consents, or if the
individual ceases employment with Provider, becomes incapacitated,
or for cause) must be with personnel of comparable seniority,
qualifications, and experience, reasonably acceptable to Client.

7.5 **Replacement at Client's request.** Client may, on reasonable
written notice, request the replacement of any Provider personnel
where the individual has demonstrated lack of fit, insufficient
performance, breach of conduct standards, or breach of Section 10
or 11. Provider will replace the individual within a reasonable
period at no extra cost. This is in addition to, not in lieu of,
any rights Client has under Section 16.

7.6 **Subcontracting.** Provider may engage Subcontractors to
perform any portion of the Services, provided that:
(a) Provider remains primarily liable for the performance of, and
the acts and omissions of, the Subcontractor as if they were
Provider's own;
(b) the Subcontractor is bound to obligations of confidentiality
and IP assignment no less protective than this Agreement; and
(c) where the Subcontractor will process Personal Data on behalf
of Client, Section 11 and the regional DPA apply.

7.7 **Pre-approved Subcontractors.** Subcontractors listed in
Annex B are deemed approved as at the Effective Date. Any new
Subcontractor for material Services not listed must be notified to
Client in advance and is subject to Client's reasonable objection.

7.8 **No solicitation.** During each SOW term and for twelve (12)
months thereafter, neither Party will knowingly and directly solicit
for employment any individual then assigned to that SOW by the
other Party. General job postings, recruiter searches not targeting
the other Party's personnel, and unsolicited applications are not
restricted, and the response of an individual to an unsolicited
opportunity is not a breach.

7.9 **Background checks.** Where the SOW requires personnel to
have a clean criminal history, drug screen, or specific clearance,
Provider will, before assignment, confirm in writing that the
individual meets the standard, subject to applicable
employment-screening law.

## 8. Acceptance & deliverables

This Section sets the procedure by which Deliverables get reviewed,
accepted, or rejected, and what happens if a Deliverable misses the
mark.

8.1 **Delivery.** Provider delivers each Deliverable to Client by
the method, format, and to the recipient identified in the SOW.
Delivery occurs on the date Client receives reasonable access to
the Deliverable in the form contracted for, accompanied by any
documentation the SOW requires.

8.2 **Acceptance window.** Client has a period of {{Acceptance Period}}
days from delivery to test the Deliverable against the Acceptance
Criteria and, in writing, either (a) accept the Deliverable or
(b) reject it, identifying with reasonable specificity the failures
of conformity.

8.3 **Deemed acceptance.** If Client neither accepts nor rejects a
Deliverable within {{Deemed Acceptance Window}} days after delivery,
the Deliverable is deemed accepted. Use of a Deliverable in
production for material business purposes also constitutes
acceptance.

8.4 **Rework on rejection.** On a timely rejection, Provider will,
at no additional charge, correct the identified non-conformities
and redeliver within a reasonable period. The Acceptance Period
restarts on redelivery. Provider has two (2) opportunities to
remediate, after which Client may, at its election:
(a) accept the Deliverable with a Fee reduction reasonable to the
remaining shortfall;
(b) terminate the affected SOW for material breach under Section
16 and recover Fees paid for the rejected Deliverable; or
(c) require a refund of Fees paid for the rejected Deliverable
without terminating the SOW, and forgo the Deliverable.

8.5 **Limits on rejection.** Client may not reject a Deliverable
for non-conformities that are immaterial, that are caused by
Client-furnished materials or directions, or that result from
Client's modification of the Deliverable post-delivery.

8.6 **Service Deliverables.** Where Services are performed without
a discrete Deliverable (e.g., advisory hours, managed support),
Section 8 does not apply and Section 4 governs the standard of
performance.

8.7 **Acceptance does not waive warranty.** Acceptance does not
waive Client's rights under the warranties in Section 12 or the
indemnities in Section 13.

8.8 **Standards in absence of specifics.** Where the SOW does not
state Acceptance Criteria, the framework set out in Annex C
applies.

## 9. Intellectual property (foreground / background / open-source)  *(DUAL-PERSPECTIVE)*

This Section allocates ownership of, and rights to use, the
materials produced under SOWs and the materials each Party brings
to the engagement.

### 9.A Client-favorable variant

9.A.1 **Foreground IP — Client owns.** All Foreground IP vests in
Client on creation. To the extent any does not so vest by law,
Provider assigns to Client, in perpetuity worldwide, all right,
title, and interest, including causes of action for past, present,
and future infringement. Provider will, at Client's expense, execute
further documents to perfect title.

9.A.2 **Moral rights.** To the maximum extent permitted by law,
Provider and its personnel waive moral rights in the Foreground IP
and consent to acts that would otherwise infringe them.

9.A.3 **Background IP — Client licence.** Provider grants Client a
perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, non-exclusive, royalty-free,
sublicensable licence to use Background IP embedded in or required
to use the Foreground IP, for Client's business and any successor.

9.A.4 **Open-source policy.** Provider may not incorporate Third-
Party Materials (including open-source) without prior written
approval; approved components must appear in an SBOM. Default
policy: {{Open Source Policy}}; copyleft components require
case-by-case approval.

9.A.5 **Generative AI output.** Provider may not use generative AI
to produce Deliverables without prior written approval. Where
approved, Provider remains responsible for originality, accuracy,
and freedom from third-party rights.

9.A.6 **Portfolio rights.** Provider may not publicly identify
Client, reproduce Deliverables in a portfolio, or describe the
Services without prior written approval.

### 9.B Provider-favorable variant

9.B.1 **Provider retains; Client gets a licence.** Provider owns
all Foreground IP. Provider grants Client a perpetual, worldwide,
non-exclusive, royalty-free, non-sublicensable (other than to
Client's Affiliates and contractors using the Deliverable in
support of Client's internal business) licence to use the
Foreground IP for Client's internal business purposes consistent
with the Services. Client has no right to resell, distribute, or
incorporate the Foreground IP into a product offered to third
parties.

9.B.2 **Background IP.** Each Party retains sole ownership of its
Background IP. Provider grants no rights in its Background IP
beyond those embedded in, and required to use, the Foreground IP
under the licence in Section 9.B.1.

9.B.3 **Tools, methods, residuals.** Provider retains all rights
in tools, methodologies, frameworks, templates, libraries, and
know-how used or developed in performing the Services, including
generalisations, abstractions, and improvements. Provider may
freely use ideas, concepts, and know-how retained in the
unaided memory of its personnel ("residuals").

9.B.4 **Open-source policy.** Provider may incorporate Third-
Party Materials, including open-source components, into
Deliverables in accordance with industry-standard practice and the
open-source policy {{Open Source Policy}}. Provider will use
reasonable efforts to record components used so that an SBOM can
be produced on request.

9.B.5 **Client-furnished materials.** Client retains ownership of
materials it provides and grants Provider a worldwide,
non-exclusive licence to use them as necessary to perform the
Services.

9.B.6 **Portfolio and case studies.** Provider may identify Client
as a customer (logo and name only) and describe in general terms
the work performed, provided no Confidential Information of Client
is disclosed.

### 9.C Market-standard variant *(default)*

9.C.1 **Foreground IP — Bespoke Deliverables assigned.** Provider
assigns to Client all right, title, and interest in Foreground IP
that is bespoke to Client's specifications and identified as a
Deliverable (the "Bespoke Deliverables"). Default posture:
{{IP Ownership Default}} where the SOW is silent.

9.C.2 **Provider's retained materials.** Provider retains ownership
of (a) its Background IP, (b) tools, frameworks, methodologies,
templates, libraries, and know-how used in but not specifically
developed for the Services, and (c) generalised improvements to
the foregoing. To the extent any of (a)–(c) is embedded in a
Bespoke Deliverable, Provider grants Client a perpetual, worldwide,
non-exclusive, royalty-free licence (sublicensable to Affiliates
and to service providers on Client's behalf, but not to third-party
purchasers as a standalone product) to use them as embedded.

9.C.3 **Background IP non-encroachment.** Each Party retains sole
ownership of its Background IP; no transfer occurs by this
Agreement.

9.C.4 **Moral rights.** To the maximum extent permitted by
applicable law, Provider and its personnel waive moral rights in
Bespoke Deliverables. Where waiver is not permitted (as in many
civil-law jurisdictions), Provider consents to reasonable Client
uses and will not assert moral rights against them.

9.C.5 **Third-Party Materials.** Provider discloses all Third-Party
Materials in a Deliverable and will not knowingly incorporate
components on terms inconsistent with Client's intended use.
Default open-source policy: {{Open Source Policy}}. Provider
supplies an SBOM on request.

9.C.6 **Generative AI output.** Provider notifies Client in writing
where generative AI tools have been materially used to produce a
Deliverable. Provider remains responsible for originality,
accuracy, and freedom from third-party rights regardless of tool.

9.C.7 **Further assurances.** Each Party will execute additional
instruments to perfect the IP allocation, at the requesting
Party's expense.

9.C.8 **Reservation.** Rights not expressly granted are reserved.

## 10. Confidentiality

This Section protects information each Party shares in the course of
the engagement and survives the end of the relationship.

10.1 **Definition.** "Confidential Information" means information
disclosed by or on behalf of one Party (the "Discloser") to the
other (the "Recipient") that is identified as confidential at
disclosure or that a reasonable recipient would understand, from
the nature of the information or the circumstances, to be
confidential. Confidential Information includes business plans,
financials, customer and supplier lists, source code, designs,
algorithms, Personal Data, and the existence and terms of this
Agreement and the SOWs.

10.2 **Exclusions.** Confidential Information does not include
information that:

(e) Section 17 applies.

16.C.7 **Survival.** Sections 2, 3.3, 5 (accrued amounts), 9, 10,

## 17. Transition assistance & wind-down  *(DUAL-PERSPECTIVE)*

This Section sets out what happens after notice of termination so
that the Services can be wound down or transitioned without
disruption to Client.

### 17.A Client-favorable variant

17.A.1 **Mandatory transition.** On notice of termination or
expiration, Provider will, on Client's request, provide transition
assistance for up to twelve (12) months to enable Client to bring
the Services in-house or transition to a successor provider.

17.A.2 **Pricing.** Transition assistance is provided at the
rates in the SOW for like work, prorated for partial periods. If
no rate card applies, then at Provider's then-current standard
rates, but in no case more than 110% of the rates in effect at
the time of termination.

17.A.3 **Scope.** Transition assistance includes, on Client's
request: knowledge transfer sessions; documentation of processes,
configurations, and customisations; training of Client or
successor-provider personnel; access to systems necessary for
hand-off; and reasonable cooperation with the successor.

17.A.4 **No leverage.** Provider may not condition transition
assistance on payment of disputed amounts, settlement of unrelated
claims, or new commitments. Withholding of Client data, source
code, or Deliverables is not permitted regardless of the reason
for termination.

17.A.5 **Data return.** Within thirty (30) days of termination,
Provider will return all Client data, Deliverables, and work
product in a commonly used machine-readable format and certify
deletion of remaining copies (subject to Section 10.7).

17.A.6 **Continuity during wind-down.** Service levels in effect
before termination remain in effect during the transition period.

### 17.B Provider-favorable variant

17.B.1 **Transition only on agreement.** Where the Parties wish to
contract for transition assistance, they will execute an SOW or
Change Order describing the scope, fees, and term. Absent such
documentation, Provider has no transition obligations beyond the
return of Client data and orderly handover of work-in-progress
under Section 16.C.6(a).

17.B.2 **Pre-payment.** Where Client requests transition
assistance after termination for cause by Provider (i.e.,
Client's breach), Provider may require pre-payment of transition
fees as a condition of performance.

17.B.3 **Scope limits.** Transition assistance does not include
the licensing of Provider tools, methods, or Background IP for
ongoing use; remediation of Client systems; or training beyond
what is reasonably needed to enable the successor provider to
operate Deliverables as delivered.

17.B.4 **No long-tail.** Transition assistance terminates no more
than ninety (90) days after the SOW termination date.

### 17.C Market-standard variant *(default)*

17.C.1 **Transition assistance.** On Client's written request,
Provider provides reasonable transition assistance for up to six
(6) months after termination or expiration (the "Transition
Period") to enable Client to bring Services in-house or transition
to a successor provider.

17.C.2 **Scope.** Transition assistance includes:
(a) knowledge-transfer sessions and documentation of processes,
configurations, customisations, and operational runbooks;
(b) cooperation with Client and any successor provider;
(c) continued performance of the Services on existing terms
during the Transition Period as Client requests;
(d) return of Client data and Deliverables in machine-readable
formats; and
(e) other reasonable assistance the Parties agree.

17.C.3 **Pricing.** Transition is charged at SOW rates for like
work, or absent that at Provider's standard rates. Orderly
close-out under Section 16.C.6(a) (data return, handover, final
invoicing) carries no transition fee.

17.C.4 **Pre-conditions.** Transition is conditional on Client's
payment of undisputed Fees due. Provider may not withhold Client
data, source code, or paid-for Deliverables as leverage on
disputed amounts or unrelated claims.

17.C.5 **Cooperation.** Each Party identifies transition
contacts and responds to reasonable requests promptly. Provider
will not solicit Client personnel known to be assigned to the
transition during the Transition Period and for six (6) months
thereafter, subject to Section 7.8.

17.C.6 **End of Transition Period.** Provider's residual
obligations are limited to (a) Section 12 warranty obligations on
accepted Deliverables, (b) Sections 10–11 confidentiality/data
obligations, and (c) any other obligations the SOW states to
survive.

## 18. Dispute resolution & governing law

This Section describes how disagreements get worked out and which
forum and law decide them.

18.1 **Good-faith negotiation.** A Party with a claim under this
Agreement will give the other Party written notice describing the
claim. The Parties' designated representatives will meet (in
person or by video) within fifteen (15) Business Days of the
notice and negotiate in good faith for a period of at least
thirty (30) days before initiating formal proceedings, except
that nothing in this Section prevents either Party from seeking
interim equitable relief at any time.

18.2 **Mediation.** If negotiation does not resolve the dispute,
the Parties will, before initiating arbitration or litigation,
participate in non-binding mediation administered by a mutually
agreed mediator. Each Party bears its own costs and shares the
mediator's fees equally. Mediation is not required where it
would prevent timely interim relief.

18.3 **Forum.** Any dispute not resolved through mediation will be
finally resolved through the forum elected in the regional
companion document. In this baseline, the Parties record two
optional defaults; they will select one at signing:
(a) **Litigation.** Exclusive jurisdiction in the courts of
{{Venue}}, applying the law of {{Governing Law}}.
(b) **Arbitration.** Final binding arbitration under the
{{Arbitration Rules}}, before a panel of three arbitrators (or
one for claims under {{Currency}} 250,000), seated in {{Venue}},
proceeding in English, and decided under the law of {{Governing
Law}}. The arbitral award may be entered in any court of competent
jurisdiction.

18.4 **No class actions.** To the maximum extent permitted by
applicable law, the Parties waive any right to participate in a
class, collective, or representative action against one another in
connection with this Agreement.

18.5 **Equitable relief.** Either Party may seek interim or
permanent equitable relief in any court of competent jurisdiction
to prevent breach of confidentiality, breach of intellectual
property rights, or other irreparable harm, without first
exhausting Sections 18.1–18.3.

18.6 **Currency of award.** Damages are awarded in {{Currency}}
unless the forum requires otherwise.

18.7 **Costs.** Each Party bears its own costs of dispute
resolution, except that the prevailing Party in any proceeding may
recover reasonable attorneys' fees and costs where the forum's
law permits.

18.8 **Limitations.** No claim arising under this Agreement may
be brought more than two (2) years after the claim accrued,
except as a longer period is mandated by applicable law or as
required for the indemnities in Section 13.

18.9 **Governing law.** This Agreement, and any non-contractual
obligations arising in connection with it, are governed by the
law of {{Governing Law}}, without regard to conflict-of-laws
principles. The U.N. Convention on Contracts for the
International Sale of Goods is excluded to the extent it might
otherwise apply.

## Annex C — Service standards & acceptance criteria framework

*Placeholder. Use this framework where the SOW does not state
specific Acceptance Criteria.*

**C.1 General standard.** A Deliverable conforms when it materially
satisfies the description in the SOW, the documentation Provider
delivered with it, and the reasonable expectations of a competent
recipient of similar Deliverables.

**C.2 Software Deliverables.** Software Deliverables are tested
against:
(a) the functional and non-functional requirements stated in the
SOW;
(b) error rates not materially exceeding industry norms for similar
software at similar maturity;
(c) operation on the environments stated in the SOW; and
(d) absence of malicious code per Section 12.C.6.

**C.3 Documents and reports.** Document Deliverables conform when
they (a) cover the topics in the SOW, (b) are accurate to the
information available to Provider as of the date of delivery, and
(c) are presented in a form suitable for the audience identified
in the SOW.

**C.4 Designs and creative.** Creative Deliverables conform when
they meet the brief, include the assets identified, and are free
of third-party rights inconsistent with the licence granted.

**C.5 Service-level Deliverables.** Where Services are provided
on a managed or recurring basis, conformity is measured against
the service levels in the SOW; remedies are limited to those
stated.

**C.6 Material vs immaterial defects.** A defect is "material" if
it (a) prevents a substantial use case stated in the SOW, (b)
materially impairs performance below the service level stated, or
(c) creates a security or compliance exposure. Other defects are
"immaterial" and do not justify rejection but may be logged for
warranty remediation under Section 12.

**C.7 Re-test.** On redelivery, the same Acceptance Criteria
apply; testing is limited to the items previously rejected and any
others reasonably indicated by the rejection.
