## 1. Plain-language summary / Yfirlit á einföldu máli

This section explains, in everyday language, what the rest of this Master Services Agreement says. The Provider (Þjónustuveitandi) agrees to perform professional or managed services for the Client (Verkkaupi) under one or more Statements of Work (Verkáætlun) attached to this framework. This Agreement records the legal and commercial baseline that applies across every engagement; each Statement of Work then carries the deal-specific particulars — scope, deliverables, fees, milestones, and key personnel.

This Agreement is service-oriented (not subscription-oriented). It is intended for B2B engagements between commercial Aðilar (Parties) established or operating in Iceland or in another EEA Member State. Where a Client qualifies as a consumer (neytandi), separate consumer-facing terms must be used. The structure follows a 19-section pattern, three annexes, and three appendices. Where an obligation is imported from EU law, that obligation reaches Iceland through the EEA Agreement and through Joint Committee decisions transposed by Althingi; each EEA-imported obligation is annotated `[VERIFY]` so a verifying lawyer can confirm current incorporation status before execution. The plain-language wrappers at the start of each numbered section are explanatory only; the operative text follows in numbered sub-clauses.

## 3. Engagement structure / Uppbygging samningssambandsins

This section describes how the documents fit together. The MSA records the standing legal terms once. Each Statement of Work or Order Form then records the deal-specific particulars. Either Party may sign more than one Statement of Work under this MSA.

3.1 **Components.** This Agreement consists of (a) these master terms; (b) each executed Statement of Work in the form of Annex A; (c) each executed Order Form; (d) Annex B (Approved subcontractors and key personnel); (e) Annex C (Service standards and acceptance criteria framework); and (f) any other addendum the Parties expressly incorporate in writing.

3.2 **Order of precedence.** In the event of conflict between documents that form part of this Agreement, the documents control in the following order: (i) an executed Statement of Work or Order Form, but only as to its own scope; (ii) any data-processing addendum executed under §11; (iii) these master terms; (iv) Annexes A, B, and C; and (v) any policy referenced by URL. A policy referenced by URL may not reduce a Client right that is granted in the master terms or in a data-processing addendum.

3.3 **Effectiveness.** This Agreement becomes effective on the Effective Date and continues in force until all Statements of Work have expired or been terminated and either Party gives written notice of termination of the master terms in accordance with §16.

3.4 **Affiliates.** A Client Affiliate may execute a Statement of Work that references this Agreement; that Affiliate becomes the contracting Client for that Statement of Work, and the entity that signed the master terms acts as guarantor of the Affiliate's payment obligations unless the Statement of Work expressly says otherwise.

3.5 **MSA-only is incomplete.** This Agreement is a framework. It does not by itself oblige the Provider to perform any work or the Client to pay any Fees. A Statement of Work or Order Form is required for each engagement.

3.6 **Approval method.** Statements of Work and Order Forms become effective when executed by both Parties using the approval method recorded as `{{SOW Approval Method}}` (e.g., countersigned PDF, purchase order with referenced terms, or qualified electronic signature). Electronic signatures that satisfy Lög nr. 28/2001 um rafræn samskipti og rafrænar undirskriftir, as amended in line with the EEA-imported eIDAS Regulation (EU) 910/2014 `[VERIFY current consolidated text]`, are deemed equivalent to wet-ink signatures.

## 4. Scope of services and SOW process / Umfang þjónustu og verkáætlunarferli

This section explains how an engagement is scoped. The Provider performs only what a signed Statement of Work calls for. The Statement of Work must describe the Services in enough detail that both sides know what "done" means.

4.1 **Scope discipline.** The Provider shall perform the Services described in each Statement of Work with reasonable skill and care, in accordance with applicable law, and in accordance with the acceptance criteria framework at Annex C. The Provider is not obliged to perform work that is not described in a Statement of Work or in a Change Order.

4.2 **SOW contents.** Each Statement of Work shall include, at a minimum: (a) a description of the Services; (b) the Deliverables (if any) and their acceptance criteria; (c) the Fees and the billing basis (fixed-fee, time and materials, milestone, or recurring); (d) the start date and the duration; (e) the named Key Personnel and the agreed engagement allocation; and (f) any Client-side dependencies on which the Provider is entitled to rely.

4.3 **Client cooperation.** The Client shall provide the Provider with timely access to the Client Materials, the Client personnel, and the Client systems that are reasonably necessary for the Provider to perform the Services. Where a Client dependency is not met by an agreed date, the Provider shall not be in default for any consequent delay and may invoke the Change Control process at §6.

4.4 **Authority.** Each Party shall designate a contract manager with day-to-day authority to administer the Agreement and the Statements of Work. The contract manager is recorded in each Statement of Work and may be changed by written notice.

4.5 **Independent contractor status.** The relationship of the Parties is that of independent contractors. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment relationship, an agency, a partnership (sameignarfélag), or a joint venture. Neither Party may bind the other to any obligation without that Party's prior written consent. The Provider shall ensure that its own employees and Subcontractors do not hold themselves out as employees, agents, or representatives of the Client.

## 5. Fees, expenses and invoicing / Þóknun, kostnaður og reikningsgerð  *(DUAL-PERSPECTIVE)*

This section explains how money flows. Fees are set on the Statement of Work or Order Form and are payable in the currency stated there. Late payments accrue Icelandic statutory interest under Lög nr. 38/2001 um vexti og verðtryggingu.

5.1 **Fees.** The Client shall pay the Fees as recorded on each Statement of Work or Order Form. Unless the document says otherwise, Fees are stated in `{{Currency}}` (default ISK; alternatives include EUR or USD where the Statement of Work expressly so provides) and are exclusive of taxes, expenses, and any third-party pass-through costs.

5.2 **Invoicing.** The Provider shall issue invoices in accordance with the Statement of Work. Unless the Statement of Work says otherwise, invoices are payable within `{{Payment Terms}}` (default Net 30) of the invoice date. Invoices shall comply with Icelandic e-invoicing recommendations published by Ríkisskattstjóri (RSK) and shall include the Provider's kennitala, the Client's kennitala, the VSK rate applied (if any), the period covered, the activity narrative, and any expense-line documentation required under §5.5 `[VERIFY current RSK e-invoicing format requirements at execution]`.

5.3 **Disputed amounts.** The Client may withhold payment of an amount it disputes in good faith, provided that it (a) pays the undisputed portion when due; (b) gives the Provider written notice of the dispute within ten (10) business days of the invoice date; and (c) cooperates in good faith to resolve the dispute. A withhold against the undisputed portion is not permitted.

5.4 **Late payment / Dráttarvextir.** Late payments accrue interest under § 6 of Lög nr. 38/2001 um vexti og verðtryggingu at the dráttarvextir rate published by Seðlabanki Íslands and applicable to commercial debts, calculated daily from the due date until payment is received `[VERIFY current Seðlabanki Íslands published dráttarvextir rate at execution]`. The Provider is also entitled to recover the cost of collection consistent with § 9 of the same Act and any related administrative fee permitted by law.

5.5 **Expenses.** The Provider may invoice pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses at cost, with VSK passed through where applicable, supported by receipts and a brief activity narrative. The Provider shall obtain the Client's prior written approval for any single expense above `{{Expense Pre-Approval Threshold}}` (default ISK 100,000) and for any travel outside the Reykjavík capital region.

5.6 **Taxes — VSK / virðisaukaskattur.** Fees are exclusive of value-added tax (virðisaukaskattur, VSK / VAT). Where Icelandic VSK is chargeable, the Provider shall add VSK at the applicable rate (currently 24% standard or 11% reduced `[VERIFY current rates under Lög nr. 50/1988 at execution]`) and issue a tax-compliant invoice consistent with Lög nr. 50/1988 um virðisaukaskatt. For cross-border B2B supplies of services within the EEA, the reverse-charge mechanism applies as transposed from the EU VAT Directive, and the Client shall account for VAT in its own jurisdiction where required. Each Party is responsible for its own income, corporation, and similar taxes.

5.7 **Withholding tax.** If the Client is required by law to withhold tax on Fees, it shall (a) gross up the payment so that the Provider receives the amount it would have received absent the withholding, except where a double-tax treaty between Iceland and the Client's jurisdiction permits a reduced rate on submission of a valid certificate of residence; and (b) provide the Provider with the official tax receipt within thirty (30) days of withholding.

5.8 **Currency.** Where the Statement of Work elects EUR or USD instead of ISK, the Parties may record an FX conversion mechanism in that document. In the absence of such a mechanism, the Statement of Work currency is the currency of payment, and currency-conversion costs lie with the paying Party.

> **Dual-perspective drafting note (§5).**
> *Client-favourable.* Extend payment term to Net 45 or Net 60; require itemised time-and-materials invoices with rate cards and individual time entries; cap annual rate-card escalation to a published Hagstofa Íslands consumer price index figure; require dráttarvextir to accrue only after a written reminder and a five-business-day grace window.
> *Provider-favourable.* Shorten payment term to Net 15 or due-on-receipt; permit interim or progress invoicing without milestone gating; add a reasonable late-payment uplift on top of statutory dráttarvextir consistent with Lög nr. 38/2001; require the Client to pay all FX and bank-transfer costs.
> *Market-standard.* Net 30 payment term; statutory dráttarvextir at the Seðlabanki Íslands published rate from the due date; reasonable cost-of-collection recovery under § 9 of the Act; annual escalation at CPI plus a stated margin where the engagement runs longer than twelve months.

## 6. Change control and change orders / Breytingarstýring  *(DUAL-PERSPECTIVE)*

This section explains how the parties handle requests to change a Statement of Work after it has been signed. Changes are made through written Change Orders, not through informal email or hallway conversations.

6.1 **Initiating a change.** Either Party may propose a change to a Statement of Work by issuing a written change request that describes the proposed change, the reason for it, and the requested implementation date.

6.2 **Provider impact assessment.** Within `{{Change Assessment Window}}` (default ten (10) business days) of receipt of a change request, the Provider shall issue a written impact assessment addressing scope, schedule, Fees, dependencies, Key Personnel, and risk. While the assessment is in progress, the Provider shall continue to perform the Services as scoped.

6.3 **Change Order.** A change becomes binding only when both Parties execute a Change Order using the approval method recorded as `{{SOW Approval Method}}`. An executed Change Order amends the underlying Statement of Work to the extent of the change, and the master terms continue to apply unchanged.

6.4 **Mandatory regulatory changes.** Where a change is required by a change in applicable law, by an order of a competent authority, or by a Persónuvernd guidance update, the Provider shall implement the change within a reasonable period and the Parties shall negotiate any related Fee adjustment in good faith. The Provider shall not be in breach for the period reasonably required to implement a mandatory change.

6.5 **Unconscionability flag (Lög nr. 7/1936 § 36).** The Parties acknowledge that under § 36 of Lög nr. 7/1936 um samninga (Samningalögin), an Icelandic court may set aside or modify a contract term, including a Change Order, if it would be unreasonable to invoke the term (ósanngjarnt að bera þá fyrir sig). The threshold for B2B unconscionability is materially higher than for B2C, but a court may still review terms governing scope reduction, Fee escalations imposed unilaterally, or extended timeline shifts adopted under economic pressure.

6.6 **Verbal changes are not binding.** A verbal request, an email confirmation absent the agreed approval method, or a Slack/Teams message is not a Change Order and does not amend the Statement of Work, regardless of the seniority of the persons involved.

> **Dual-perspective drafting note (§6).**
> *Client-favourable.* Cap permitted Fee uplift per Change Order at a stated percentage; require the Provider to absorb up to a stated number of hours of minor changes per quarter without charge; require that any change to Key Personnel composition needs Client written approval before the Change Order is binding.
> *Provider-favourable.* Require the Client to pay for the impact assessment itself where the request is not adopted; permit the Provider to suspend related work pending Change Order signature; add a small administrative fee per Change Order to cover overhead.
> *Market-standard.* Written Change Order required, ten-business-day impact-assessment window, Fees and timeline adjusted on a rate-card basis, Key Personnel changes require notice but not consent except for named individuals.

## 8. Acceptance and deliverables / Samþykki og afurðir

This section explains what happens when the Provider hands over a Deliverable. The Client gets a defined window to review and to either accept or reject. If the Client says nothing, the Deliverable is deemed accepted after the deemed-acceptance window.

8.1 **Delivery.** The Provider shall deliver each Deliverable in accordance with the Statement of Work and with the acceptance criteria framework at Annex C.

8.2 **Acceptance period.** The Client has `{{Acceptance Period}}` (default fifteen (15) business days) from delivery to test the Deliverable against the acceptance criteria and to give written notice of acceptance or written notice of rejection identifying the specific deficiency.

8.3 **Deemed acceptance.** Where the Client does not give a written notice of acceptance or rejection within `{{Deemed Acceptance Window}}` (default twenty (20) business days) of delivery, the Deliverable is deemed accepted. Use of the Deliverable in production by the Client also constitutes acceptance, except where the use is required to test the Deliverable.

8.4 **Cure of rejected Deliverable.** Where the Client rejects a Deliverable for failure to meet the acceptance criteria, the Provider shall, at no additional charge, correct the deficiency within a reasonable period and re-submit. If the Provider fails to deliver a substantially conforming Deliverable after two cure attempts, the Client may, at its election, (a) accept the non-conforming Deliverable with an equitable Fee adjustment; (b) terminate the affected Statement of Work and recover the Fees paid for that Deliverable; or (c) escalate under §18.

8.5 **No waiver.** Acceptance of a Deliverable does not waive any warranty in §12 or any indemnity in §13.

8.6 **Service-level engagements.** Where the Statement of Work is for ongoing services rather than discrete Deliverables, acceptance is replaced by service-level measurement against the standards recorded in Annex C and any service-level addendum.

## 9. Intellectual property / Hugverkaréttindi  *(DUAL-PERSPECTIVE)*

This section explains who owns what. The default in this template is `{{IP Ownership Default}}` — typically Client owns the Foreground IP in the Deliverables once paid, while the Provider keeps the Background IP. Icelandic copyright and moral-rights rules require some care; the rules are not identical to the United States or to the United Kingdom.

9.1 **Background IP.** Each Party retains all right, title, and interest in and to its own Background IP. Nothing in this Agreement transfers Background IP between the Parties.

9.2 **Background IP licence.** Where the Provider's Background IP is incorporated into, or is required to use, a Deliverable, the Provider grants the Client, on payment of the related Fees, a non-exclusive, perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free licence to use, copy, modify, and distribute that Background IP to the extent necessary for the Client's use of the Deliverable for the Client's internal business purposes. The licence is sublicensable to the Client's Affiliates and to its outsourcing service providers acting on the Client's behalf.

9.3 **Foreground IP — assignment (default).** Subject to §§ 9.4 and 9.5, the Provider, on payment in full of the Fees for the relevant Statement of Work, assigns to the Client all right, title, and interest in and to the Foreground IP that is conceived, developed, or first reduced to practice specifically for the Client in the course of performing the Services. The assignment is by way of present-tense conveyance to the maximum extent permitted by Icelandic law, with the Provider undertaking to execute any further document reasonably necessary to perfect the assignment.

9.4 **Höfundalög — Icelandic copyright defaults.** The Parties acknowledge that under Lög nr. 73/1972 um höfundarétt (Höfundalög):

  (a) **No automatic vesting.** A commissioning relationship does not by itself vest copyright in the commissioner. An express written assignment is required for the Client to take copyright ownership.

  (b) **Sæmdarréttur — moral rights (§ 3).** The economic rights of an author may be assigned, but the moral rights of attribution and integrity (sæmdarréttur) are inalienable. They may, however, be waived in writing for individual specific uses. Each individual creator who contributes to a Deliverable shall, where reasonably required by the Client, sign a written waiver of sæmdarréttur in respect of the agreed specific uses.

  (c) **Software — § 11 default.** Where computer software is created by an employee in the course of employment, the employer is the default owner of the economic rights, consistent with § 11 of Höfundalög and the EEA-imported Software Directive.

  (d) **Database right.** Where a Deliverable qualifies as a database within the meaning of § 50 of Höfundalög (transposing the EU Database Directive), the sui generis database right vests in the maker; the Parties intend that, where the Client is the maker by reason of substantial investment, the database right vests in the Client.

9.5 **Trade secrets.** Trade secrets (uppskriftir) are protected under Lög nr. 16/2016 um vernd uppskrifta (Iceland's transposition of EU Directive 2016/943 on trade secrets). Each Party undertakes to handle the other Party's trade secrets in accordance with that Act, and the additional remedies available under that Act are preserved.

9.6 **Open source.** Where Foreground IP incorporates open-source software, the Provider shall comply with the open-source policy recorded as `{{Open Source Policy}}` (e.g., permissive only; any with notice; Client pre-approval). The Provider shall provide an open-source bill of materials on request, listing each component, version, and licence. The Provider warrants that no copyleft licence (e.g., GPL, AGPL, or LGPL) has been incorporated into a Deliverable in a manner that would, on its face, propagate copyleft terms to the Deliverable as a whole, except as expressly approved in writing.

9.7 **Reservation.** Except as expressly granted in this section, no licence is granted by either Party.

> **Dual-perspective drafting note (§9).**
> *Client-favourable.* Broaden the Foreground IP assignment to include all Provider know-how reasonably embedded in the Deliverable; require the Provider to procure signed sæmdarréttur waivers from each individual creator before delivery; cap the Provider's reservation of Background IP to items listed in an attached schedule.
> *Provider-favourable.* Narrow the Foreground IP transfer to a non-exclusive licence rather than an assignment; reserve the right to reuse generic methodologies, frameworks, and patterns; refuse to procure individual sæmdarréttur waivers and instead rely on a corporate undertaking not to enforce moral rights.
> *Market-standard.* Provider assigns Client-specific Foreground IP on payment; Provider keeps Background IP and grants a use licence; Provider warrants no embedded copyleft; Provider procures sæmdarréttur waivers for delivery uses but not for arbitrary downstream Client uses.

## 10. Confidentiality / Trúnaður

This section requires each Party to keep the other's non-public information secret. Confidential information may only be used to perform the Agreement and may not be shared except as the Agreement allows.

10.1 **Obligations.** Each Party (the "Recipient") shall (a) use the other Party's Confidential Information only to perform this Agreement; (b) protect it with at least the degree of care it uses for its own confidential information of similar importance, and never less than reasonable care; and (c) limit access to those of its personnel, advisers, and Subcontractors who need to know and who are bound by written confidentiality obligations no less protective than this section.

10.2 **Exclusions.** Confidential Information does not include information that the Recipient can show is (a) publicly available without breach of this Agreement; (b) already known to the Recipient without confidentiality restriction before disclosure; (c) independently developed by the Recipient without use of the other Party's Confidential Information; or (d) lawfully obtained from a third party without confidentiality restriction.

10.3 **Compelled disclosure.** If the Recipient is required by law, court order, or competent authority to disclose Confidential Information, it shall (where lawful) give the disclosing Party prompt notice and reasonable cooperation so the disclosing Party may seek a protective order.

## Annex C — Service standards and acceptance criteria framework / Þjónustustig og samþykkisviðmið

This Annex sets the framework that Statements of Work draw on for service-level standards and acceptance criteria.

C.1 **Service-level standards (managed services).** Standard categories: availability target; response-time targets by severity (P1–P4); resolution-time targets by severity; service hours; out-of-hours coverage; reporting cadence; service credit framework. C.2 **Acceptance criteria categories.** Standard categories: functional conformity to written specification; non-functional performance benchmarks (e.g., response time, throughput); security baseline (e.g., OWASP ASVS Level 2 or equivalent, NIS2-aligned where applicable); accessibility (e.g., WCAG 2.2 AA); documentation completeness; open-source bill of materials accuracy.

C.4 **Defect categories.** Critical (production blocker); High (material function broken); Medium (workaround available); Low (cosmetic). Acceptance is permitted with a defined number of Medium / Low defects on a remediation schedule, but no Critical or High open defects.

C.5 **Reporting.** A standing monthly written status report is the default cadence for managed services and for engagements longer than three months. Recurring meetings supplement, but do not replace, the written report.
